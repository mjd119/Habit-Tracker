Understanding Variable Types and Measurements

There are a few different types of variables you should be aware of when you are thinking of your research question. I use exercise as an example

Variable Type Explanations

Metric

A metric variable has an infinite number of possible values. For example, if you wanted to know how exercise affects your sleep quality, you could report 30 minutes, 32.5 minutes, 70.666 minutes, etc. of exercise.

Ordinal

An ordinal variable is a range of possible values. For example, you could rate your quality of exercise on a scale of 1-5 (worst [1], ok [2], neutral [3], good [4], best [5]). For the purposes of this website, you will report a value from 1 to 5.

Binary

A binary variable is a variable with only two possible options. For example, you could report that you did exercise (1) or did not exercise (0) today. Alternatively, you could report your exercise as "Good" (1) or "Bad" (0).

Categorical

Variable with categories. Categories could be cardio, weight-lifting, biking, etc. You are not allowed to choose this type when creating your design.
