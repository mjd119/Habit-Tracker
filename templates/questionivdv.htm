After you identify your independent and dependent variables you will choose how you plan to measure them. Variables can often be measured in many different ways. For example, if your independent variable is exercise, you could measure it as a binary variable - whether you did vs. did not go to the gym today. Alternatively, you could measure it as a 5-level ordinal variable from "No exercise" to "Tons of Exercise." Or, if you decide to measure it as a metric variable you could measure the minutes of exercise at the gym. All of these have different pros and cons; you should think about what measurement system makes the most sense for you and your particular question.

Whatever question you choose it is important that you actually engage in these activities or plan to start to engage in these activities. For example, if your question is: "Does meditation reduce my stress?" it is important that you actually medtiate somewhat frequently.
