The goal of this website is to teach you how to identify possible causal relationships in your everyday life.

In this tutorial, you will create a research question, identify your independent variables and dependent variables, measure those variables over three weeks, and draw conclusions from your data.

Each day, you will receive a text message and login to the website to record your data.

At the end of the study, you will revisit your research question and draw conclusions from your experiences.

Click the button below to start creating your research question.

Create a Research Question

Over the next 21 days, you'll try to identify possible causal relationships in your everyday life. Think about a question you want to answer. Do you want to know if meditation affects your stress, or if caffeine affects your sleep quality?

You should phrase this like a scientist would phrase a research question.

If you want to change your research question later on, you will have the opportunity to do so at the end or you can go back at any time

There is a wide range of questions you could answer by reporting on everyday events in your life

Report Examples:

  • Calories you ate
  • Quality of sleep the night before
  • Length of most recent exercise session
  • General mood throughout the day
  • Stress Levels
  • Whether or not you spent time with friends
  • Length of time spent outside
  • Screen time (e.g. phone, computer, etc.)
  • Caffeine intake (e.g. cups of coffee, cans of soda, etc.)
  • Music listening (listened to music or not)



Question Examples:

  • Does exercise improve my sleep?
  • Does meditation improve my mood?
  • Does exercise improve my mood?
  • Does extra sleep improve my mood?
  • Does listening to music reduce my anxiety levels?
  • Does socializing with my friends improve my mood?
  • Does meditation improve my conentration in class?
