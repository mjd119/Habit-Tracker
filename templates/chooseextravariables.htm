Choose Extra Variables

Create extra variables based on your research question. Choose factors you think impact your Dependent Variable. Adding a fourth and fifth variable are optional.

Whenever you study the relationship between an independent variable and a dependent variable,, there are always other variables that could influence the dependent variable. For example, if you are studying the relation between exercise and sleep, coffee, stress, and pain could also influence sleep. These variables are sometimes called "variableDivArrays" or "Alternative Causes."

On this screen you must select at at least one alternative cause. If you think there are are other alternative causes that are likely to influence your dependent variable, then please add up to two more. Remember, your goal is to figure out whether your independent variable actually has an effect on your dependent variable, so it is important to record and control for other variables that might influence your dependent variable.

Example: Can I improve my sleep? (Research Question) I think exercise will worsen my sleep (Independent Variable). I will measure hours of sleep (Dependent Variable). I think stress also worsens my sleep (Third Variable) along with caffeine intake (Another Third Variable).

You can choose up to 3 additional variables, but you need to create at least 1.
